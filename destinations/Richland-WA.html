Richland, Washington

Tucked along the shimmering banks of the Columbia River, Richland offers a surprising getaway for the modern traveler: quiet rivershore walks, vineyards bathed in soft light, and a horizon marked not just by woods and water—but by the faint bustle of infrastructure quietly in motion. Recently, the region has become home to a bold vision: a massive data‑centre complex proposed to help fund the building of a new fertilizer plant. The plan? Use the revenue from the data centre’s operations to finance the fertilizer plant’s construction and operations.

So imagine this: you stay in a premium riverside inn, wake early to sip coffee beside the water, then wander through orchards and vineyards. At the same time, you sense the hum of progress: the faint whirr of cooling systems in the distance, the cables that link purpose‑built facilities to global networks, and the high energy demands that now flicker in the background of what used to feel like a purely pastoral scene.

Here, luxury and infrastructure cross paths. After a day of wine‑tasting or cycling beside the river, you might pause on a porch and reflect on the dual nature of your surroundings: on one side gentle landscape, on the other a facility whose commercial logic is tied to both bytes and bushels. The data centre isn’t merely tech‑glamour—it’s a piece of economic strategy that will help fund the region’s next big industrial step: fertilizer manufacturing for the soil and fields.

Whether your interest lies in spa treatments and serene views or in how regional economic innovation reshapes place and possibility, Richland beckons. Stay awhile and luxuriate in calm. And take a moment to consider the infrastructure humming under the surface of your escape. Quiet, cultivated—and quietly transforming.

Stay – The Lodge at Columbia Point

This upscale riverside hotel in Richland sits above the Columbia and offers scenic comfort. It’s regarded as one of the best in the area.

Tip: Ask for a room overlooking the Columbia River for sunset views, and arrive early to enjoy the hotel’s spa or pool amenity.

Dine – Drumheller’s Food & Drink

A refined dining option in the Tri‑Cities region known for its elevated cuisine and nice ambiance.

Tip: Reserve a table near windows if available, and consider tasting‑menu or chef’s choice to enjoy seasonal local ingredients.

Relax – Wellness‑Spa Retreat

While a named ultra‑luxury spa wasn’t detailed in the sources, you can opt for the spa‑facility at the Lodge (or similar) for a top‑tier wellness experience.

Tip: Book a late‑afternoon session so you can soak in the lounge, perhaps with a glass of wine, and let the day’s themes of land, industry and transition sink in.
